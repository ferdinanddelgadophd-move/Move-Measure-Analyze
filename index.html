What Are Force Plates?

The technology behind data-driven performance testing.

Force plates are precision instruments that measure the forces your body produces during movement ‚Äî like jumping, landing, or pushing. Think of them as a highly sensitive scale that captures not just how much force you generate, but how you generate it.

Power & Explosiveness

How quickly you can generate force ‚Äî critical for sprinting, jumping, and change of direction.

Strength

Your ability to produce maximum force, similar to a 1-rep max but measured more precisely.

Reactive Ability

How well you absorb and redirect force ‚Äî essential for agility, stability, and injury prevention.

Asymmetries

Differences between your left and right sides that may indicate injury risk or limit performance.

This data helps guide your training program, track progress over time, and make informed decisions about return-to-sport readiness.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a testing session take?

For individuals, about 30‚Äì60 minutes depending on the assessments. For teams of 20 athletes with 2 assessments each, expect 1.5‚Äì2 hours (excluding warm-up time).

What space or equipment do you need at our facility?

A flat, hard surface for the force plates is essential. If we're doing isometric testing, access to a weight room with racks is needed. For drop/depth jumps, we'll need boxes or a platform. We'll discuss specifics during the consultation.

What should athletes wear?

Normal workout clothes that allow unrestricted movement, plus sneakers. Ideally, athletes should wear the same shoes they'd use for training or competition.

How soon do we get results?

Individual athlete reports are delivered within 48 hours and include detailed analysis and recommendations. For teams, we schedule a follow-up call to walk through the results together rather than just sending a stack of PDFs.

Do you travel outside New Hampshire?

Yes, with travel fees applied beyond 15 miles from Durham, NH. For recurring sessions outside NH, we'd need to register the business in that state, which we can discuss.

Can we retest athletes later to track progress?

Absolutely ‚Äî that's the real value of these assessments. Retesting allows you to measure the impact of training interventions and track development over time.

What's the difference between individual and team reports?

Individual athletes receive 4 assessments with in-depth analysis, detailed written interpretation, and comprehensive training recommendations. Team athletes receive 2 assessments with streamlined reports focused on key metrics ‚Äî coaches then get a follow-up call to discuss the full team picture. If a team athlete needs deeper analysis, they can book an individual session.

What ages or skill levels do you work with?

Athletes 13 and older. Athletes under 18 require parent/guardian permission. Younger athletes may be included if we determine the assessment would be beneficial for their development.
